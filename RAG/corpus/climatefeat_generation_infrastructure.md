## Supply-Demand Context

Total operating generation capacity in California is approximately **86,403 MW**. This represents nameplate capacity, not firm dispatchable capacity — solar (24,486 MW) and wind (6,479 MW) contribute variably depending on conditions.

For comparison with ClimateFEAT peak demand projections:
- ClimateFEAT projects statewide peak demand using top-1% daily peak hourly load across 58 counties
- The CEC's 2025 IEPR projects CAISO 1-in-2 peak reaching 66,318 MW by 2040 (Planning + Known Loads)
- CAISO actual historical peak demand ranged 43,800–51,500 MW (2014–2024)
- Current operating capacity of 86,403 MW includes ~30,965 MW of variable renewables that may not be available at peak
