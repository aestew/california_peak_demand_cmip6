Methodology
Three-step inference pipeline

No published precedent exists for this approach. ClimateFEAT chains high-resolution reanalysis, statistical downscaling, and deep learning into a single pipeline from raw climate data to county-level peak demand projections.

01 — Spatial foundation

URMA reanalysis

2.5 km gridded weather reanalysis provides the historical training foundation. Population-weighted using WorldPop to convert grid cells to county-level thermal exposure — capturing where people actually live, not just where weather stations sit.

URMA 2.5km · WorldPop · xarray · Dask
02 — Climate projection

LOCA2-Hybrid downscaling

25 ensemble members across 7 CMIP6 global climate models, statistically downscaled to match URMA resolution. Two scenarios — SSP2-4.5 (moderate) and SSP3-7.0 (high warming) — provide the range of plausible futures through 2040.

LOCA2-Hybrid · 7 GCMs · SSP2-4.5 · SSP3-7.0
03 — Demand forecasting

Multi-stream transformer

Five specialized input streams feed a two-phase attention architecture — within-stream self-attention captures domain patterns, then cross-stream attention learns how weather, infrastructure, and demographics interact to drive peak demand.

PyTorch · MLflow · county embeddings · 2-phase attention
Architecture
Five streams, one forecast

Why multi-stream?

Peak electricity demand isn't driven by any single factor. It emerges from the interaction of extreme heat, population growth, EV adoption curves, datacenter buildout, and the built environment's thermal inertia.

The five-stream architecture treats each domain as a separate token sequence. County embeddings are prepended as learned context vectors, allowing the model to capture county-specific demand signatures that a flat feature vector would miss.

Cross-stream attention then lets the model learn the interactions — how a heatwave's impact on demand differs in a county with high EV penetration versus one with expanding datacenter load.

Input streams

  • Weather + Time — Temperature, humidity, solar radiation, wind, temporal encoding
  • Rolling + Time — 3/7/14-day rolling aggregations capturing thermal accumulation
  • Geography / Demographics — Population density, urban fraction, elevation, climate zone
  • Heatwave — Duration, intensity, and nighttime recovery metrics
  • Infrastructure + EV + Time — Datacenter capacity, BEV adoption trajectories, grid infrastructure
